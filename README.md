# Project Overview
The Division 2, with its looter shooter style gameplay, could do with more player choice and decision making opertunities in combat. My Mechanic, the "Guided Drone" adds just that.
# Project Choice Rational
In researching The Division 2 for this module I bought and playtested the game. Though it is not my usual type of game, I tried my best to experence it from a perspective of a fan of the looter shooter style gameplay. In this testing was when I started to think that the fights felt very one dimentional, (just upset everyone ever) like i'm missing some kind of tactical setup or plan. The rest of the game is supposed to feel very realistic, but once you get to an area of enemies, that realism seems to slip away as all of the health bars and icons appear. Enemies never slip away, they seem to usually fall in one by one with no consideration for their life. I feel like they should really hide more (drone also becomes more impactful) and coordinate more of their attacks with surprising the player at other angles in mind... instead of flowing into one doorway to be mowed down...     |||||    With this updated enemy AI, their positions should become unpredicatible and harder to read, but this is all ok since the main mechanic I will create will be the "Guided Drone" which will serve as the player's way of keeping track of enimes if, and only if, they correctly guessed where they were going to be a few moments ago.

# Using the Guided Drone ( singleplayer only for balencing )
Upon use, the player will get out a tablet that the camera will focus on. This screen will show a grainy fizzling birds eye view of the surrounding area that the player can click and drag to draw a path for the drone to follow. Once the path has been drawn the player can choose points in the path to take a photo in a chosen direction (scan the enemies). Once the path and photo points have been selected, the player will be able to send the drone off to begin following the path. At this point the player can move and do their normal combat gameplay while the drone follows it's path. The more photos the drone takes on a single path, the higher the chance for the enemies to notice its minor flashes and shoot it down. Each photo that successfully captures an enemy will display them through walls and on the minimap for a short diration.

My hope with this drone is to take the tactical aproach of covering each others blindspots of The Division 2's multiplayer mode, and use this way to mirror it to the singleplayer experience.
