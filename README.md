# Project Overview

The Division 2, with its looter shooter style gameplay, could do with more player choice and decision making opportunities in combat. My Mechanic, the "Guided Drone" adds just that.

# Project Choice Rational

In researching The Division 2 for this module I bought and play-tested the game. Though it is not my usual type of game, I tried my best to experience it from the perspective of a fan of the looter shooter style gameplay. In this testing was when I started to think that the fights felt a bit one dimensional, like I'm missing some kind of tactical setup or plan. The rest of the game is supposed to feel very realistic, but once you get to an area of enemies, that realism seems to fade as enemies never slip away and they seem to usually come out of cover one by one with no consideration for their life. I feel like they should really hide more and coordinate more of their attacks with surprising the player with other angles in mind. For this reason, along with giving more use to the guided drone, one of my features will be a changed enemy AI that prefers to attack with their team and flank the player. With this updated enemy AI, their positions should become unpredictable and harder to read, this being all intentional to boost the feel of the main mechanic I will create, being the "Guided Drone", which will serve as the player's way of keeping track of enemies if, and only if, they correctly chose an area where they are/will be.

# Using the Guided Drone ( single-player only for balancing )

Purchased as a skill, under the drone variants. Upon use, the player will get out a tablet that the camera will focus on. This screen will show a grainy fizzling birds eye view of the surrounding area that the player can click and drag to draw a path for the drone to follow. Once the path has been drawn the player can choose points in the path to take a photo in a chosen direction (scan the enemies). Once the path and photo points have been selected, the player will send the drone off to begin following the path. At this point the player can move and do any of their normal combat gameplay while the drone follows it's path. The more photos the drone takes on a single path, the higher the chance for the enemies to notice it's minor flashes and shoot it down. Each photo that successfully frames an enemy will display them through walls and on the mini-map for a short duration.



This should provide some level of skill expression to the player's positional awareness, which has previously been left out. My hope with this drone is to take the tactical approach of covering each others blind spots in The Division 2's multiplayer mode into the single-player experience.
